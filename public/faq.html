What are SIP Trunks?

SIP Trunks are traditional Phone Lines delivered over the Internet. A SIP Trunk is the connection from your Internet enabled device to the traditional phone network.

What are the advantages of SIP Trunking rather than ISDN?

SIP Trunking is cheaper than ISDN and has a number of features that physical connections cannot provide, such as automatic failover and multi-rate center numbering.

Who can use SIP Trunking? Who are the ideal customers?

SIP Trunking can be used for any voice application, from Hosted PBX to Call Center. Customers who are using traditional voice services will see the most savings by converting to SIP, typically in the 60-80% range.

Do I need any additional equipment to use SIP Trunking?

Most Legacy Phone Systems have built-in SIP Trunking capabilities, but some older models may require an adapter. Check with your manufacturer or our Forums for more details. Also, some manufacturers requires licensing to use SIP trunking, such as Avaya or Cisco. You’ll need to work with your Avaya or Cisco reseller to ensure you have the proper licensing or SIP Trunking will not work.

Do I need more bandwidth to use SIP Trunking?

Each simultaneous call requires 64-100kilobits per second of free bandwidth. If you have a DSL line that’s 6 megabits down and 1 megabit up, you can have at least 10 calls if no other traffic is running over the network.

How many SIP Trunks do I need?

Well that depends! You can have as many DIDs as you require for your service. The total number of simultaneous calls that can be made or received will be controlled by the number of trunks. They can be made to different DIDs or all to the same.

Are SIP Trunks expensive to implement?

SIP Trunking is much cheaper than traditional voice over time. The cost of implementation is generally an hour of your time and nothing more. In rare instances, an adapter will need to be installed, but even that process won’t take more than an hour.

Are SIP Trunks reliable enough?

SIP Trunking is used by major organizations all over the world. It is the backbone of Apple’s Facetime, Google’s Hangouts and most modern Communications Services. SIP is reliable enough to be trusted by the world’s most famous brands and it is used all over the world by leading organizations.

PBXs

Can I keep my existing PBX Hardware?

Yes. Older models may need an adapter, but most PBX hardware made in the last 10 years supports SIP.

What systems are compatible with Trunking.io?

Any VoIP system is compatible with Trunking.io.

How do I register my PBX to the Trunking.io server?

Trunking.io provides an IP address and authentication credentials to you, which are input into your equipment.

Where can I find configuration instructions for my new service?

Configuration is automatic––instructions can be found in the Trunking.io Wiki.

What network ports do I need to forward to my PBX to make your service work?

Signaling takes place over TCP ports 5060 and 5061. UDP Traffic can take place over a wide range of ports, in the 16000-37000 range.

Where do I find set-up tutorial/documentations?

Please visit the Trunking.io Wiki.

What if my PBX goes down?

If your PBX goes down, Trunking.io will automatically reroute your calls to another phone number or PBX of your choice. It’s as simple as typing in the number you’d like to reroute to in event of an emergency and then sitting back and relaxing.

What codec does Trunking.io use?

Trunking.io supports most major audio codecs. Specifically:
- G.711 ulaw/alaw
- G.722 and 722.1
- G.729 (License Required)
- Siren 16/32/64khz

Can you help me with internal configurations of my phone system?

Our support is limited to helping you get configured with our SIP service. Because of the complexities and differences between customer environments, we are not able to help you configure your internal networking, firewalls and other related items. We offer professional support through network affiliations with our reseller network. Email us at support@trunking.io for inquiries.

Porting

Can I port a number?

Yes, Ports are $5/number.

How long does it take to port a number?

(Description might be different according to the GUI porting popup) Once you have service with us you can fill out this Porting form to port your existing non-toll free number(s) to us. Once filled out you should scan it along with the requested bill copies and email it back to support@trunking.io (See comments below concerning toll free service availability). The length of time is typically between 2-4 weeks for most number ports and is controlled primarily by your existing carrier.

How can I port a fax number to your service?

Faxing is always done on a best effort basis and SIP/VoIP has plenty of known issues with faxing, so you will want to make sure it works for you over our service. We recommend that you confirm the quality is acceptable before proceeding with a port request. We support t.38 for faxing, but please test everything. You should configure your trunk to use the G711 (ulaw) or t.38 codec for faxing. If you choose to proceed with a port, just follow the directions for any other number port.

How can I port toll free numbers to your service?

Yes we have toll free numbers and we accept toll free ports. Please fill out both the Responsible Organization form and the Porting form.

How do I contact for support?

Our support is delivered through email at support@trunking.io. You should provide us with your account information such as you sip username when contacting us. If you have a call issue, grabbing the call ID from the GUI will greatly expedite troubleshooting. We are very responsive on support requests.
